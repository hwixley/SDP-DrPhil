Team management

The team was split into three subteams - software, hardware, marketing. Each subteam had a leader primarily as a point of contact as tasks were assigned on a indiviudal basis rather than to a team. As seen above, most members had a main role and subrole/s.

The offline team communications took place through Slack on work spaces organized based on the departments. Slack served as a tool for making all offline decisions through polls and other means, as well as a forum to discuss non-essential decisions. All online meetings took place on our private Teams channel, thanks to which a history and lengths of meetings could be kept in one place. We used Teams to organise and place new documents in a structured way in folders for us to access later on if needed.

GitHub was used to share and version our code-base, and Trello was used to assign and keep track of all the available sub tasks and progress. These tools were chosen since all of the members have used them in the past and are familiar with them.

Meetings

Every week on Friday at 2pm the team holds full team meetings where progress will be evaluated against the master plan, and the plan adjusted if need be. Other meetings were be organised on an as-needed-basis.
